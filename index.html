Executive Summary

We're at an inflection point in global development and AI ‚Äì the next two years may have an outsized effect on the next century of impact.

Today's AI applications predominantly focus on advanced economies. Access and applications in LMICs have been largely neglected. Exciting work is happening to develop LMIC-focused interventions, but it's often in pilot stage and more resources are needed to bring this work to scale.

Leveraging AI, low & middle-income countries could "leapfrog" traditional stages of development and drive massive efficiencies. Mobile money in Kenya demonstrates this power: M-Pesa leveraged high mobile phone penetration to offer a new model for banking access, increasing financial inclusion for 30M+ people. Today, 80% of Kenyans use mobile money.

Key Takeaways

Weather forecasting for smallholder farmers appears particularly impactful and tractable, with potential to be delivered to tens of millions of farmers at extremely low marginal cost:

  • Impactful: AI models provide greater accuracy than traditional regional models, offer more precise forecasts, and unlock new types of predictions
  • Cost-Effective: Marginal costs are low (a few cents per SMS message) with high expected benefit ratio
  • Tractable: Pilots are already demonstrating early successes

Case Study: AI-Enabled Weather Forecasts for Smallholder Farmers

Delivering tailored forecasts to smallholder farmers can have transformative economic impacts:

  • Farmers in Benin and Colombia saw $100-350/year in financial benefit via SMS forecasts
  • Indian farmers gained ~$334m through improved seasonal rainfall forecasts
  • Forecasts can be delivered for pennies per farmer via SMS ($0.00019 in India, $0.03 in Ethiopia)

AI can generate more accurate, scalable forecasts across multiple variables and is cheaper to run than traditional forecasts requiring supercomputing power.

Key Takeaways

The Problem: People in LMICs face enormous gaps in healthcare access and quality. Over half the global population (about 4.5 billion people) was not fully covered by essential health services as of 2021, and there is expected to be a global shortfall of 10 million health workers by 2030.

In the near to medium term, high potential areas include:

  • AI for TB medical imaging/screening: Strong evidence supporting accuracy of Computer Aided Detection of chest X-rays for TB. ~3.1M TB cases go undiagnosed annually due to diagnostic gaps
  • AI for mental health: Potential for rules-constrained chatbots to help fill massive gaps in mental health services, though fully generative AI therapists remain too risky at this stage
  • Clinical validation needed: Most results come from simulations or small pilots. More evidence needed on integration into LMIC clinical settings with limited digital infrastructure

Important: Models are becoming more capable, but require investments in clinical validation prior to scaling in LMICs. While technology appears promising, more evidence is needed on effective integration into clinical settings with limited digital infrastructure.

Education

AI Lesson Planning Generate curriculum-aligned lesson plans and subject matter guidance Medium

Key Takeaways

Education in LMICs faces significant challenges: 70% of 10-year-olds in LMICs were unable to understand a simple written text in 2022. Teachers face large classes, limited training, and infrastructure gaps.

RCTs have validated AI education tools:

  • AI 1:1 tutoring in Ghana and Nigeria found improvements of 1-2 years of schooling after just a few months of use
  • RCT in Brazil found AI feedback increased essay scores significantly across 19K students
  • Sierra Leone study showed frequent system use by teachers

Tractability Considerations: Successfully scaling requires widespread smartphone/tablet availability, engagement with teacher unions, and consideration of AI-driven labor market shifts on education curricula.

Global Health R&D

Traditional drug development: Requires 12-15 years and ~$2.8 billion per approved drug, with only 7.9% clinical trial success rate. Novel therapies for neglected diseases are highly under-invested relative to their impact.

AI Transformation Across the R&D Value Chain

Discovery & Targeting

Medium Readiness

AI analyzes biomedical datasets to understand disease biology and identify drug candidates. Drug repurposing holds particular promise. However, applications limited by dataset quality and NTD disease complexity.

Preclinical Development

Medium Readiness

AI improves in vitro testing accuracy and "virtual patients" have matched/surpassed some animal tests. FDA now encourages AI use as animal testing replacement. Applications still nascent for complex diseases with limited data.

Clinical Testing Infrastructure

Medium Readiness

AI can optimize patient recruitment, create synthetic control arms, and improve protocol design. Most transformative applications face high data barriers in LMICs where datasets are limited.

  • Should we temper expectations given NTD complexity?
  • Which actors are best positioned for market-shaping investments?

Early Warning Weather Forecasting - High Readiness Big Bet

The Problem: About 30% of the global population lacks early-warning coverage for severe weather, leading to widespread, preventable loss of life and assets.

AI Benefits:

  • Improved accuracy and lead times (e.g., 7 days advance flood warnings vs. 1-2 days traditional)
  • Analyzes wide range of data without highly skilled workforce
  • Reduces false alarms and builds trust in evacuation recommendations

Impact Potential: Investing $800M in early warning systems in LMICs would avoid losses of $3-16BN annually.

Critical Success Factors:

  • Convincing users to trust forecasts and take recommended actions
  • Investment in quality training data for LMIC contexts
  • Last-mile delivery to ensure warnings reach vulnerable populations

Measurement & Evaluation

AI Intervention Description Readiness
Automated M&E systems Support NGOs/governments through entire M&E cycle Medium

Key Takeaways

Problem: Traditional program evaluation in LMICs is expensive and slow, requiring specialists and large teams. This delays the ability to identify needs, assess effectiveness, and adapt programs.

AI Opportunity for End-to-End M&E:

  • LLMs advise on evaluation design
  • AI voice agents and chatbots collect data through phones in local languages
  • AI tools clean, analyze, and report findings
  • Could dramatically lower costs and increase quality of evaluations

Considerations: There's no clear market failure here ‚Äì private incentives might drive this technology forward. Phone-based data collection using AI is largely untested in high-poverty regions, and many poorest households lack phone access.

Cross-Sector Hypotheses for Next Phase

We've identified hypotheses that apply across sectors for further consideration in the next phase of analysis:

Product Vision & Roadmap

  1. Think big but start small: Identify biggest overall opportunity areas and build a roadmap that stacks products over time, starting with near-term wins
  2. Prioritize user adoption: Leading indicators are more immediately measurable than multi-year RCTs, crucial amidst rapid AI change
  3. Balance breadth vs depth: Consider tradeoffs between maximizing product reach and improving outcomes for smaller user groups

Paths to Scale / Demand Drivers

  1. Governments as key path: Focus on tractability concerns and potential upsides to drive demand and scale
  2. Competition from free LLMs: Alternative demand drivers (institutions, governments) may be more tractable, or partnerships with AI Labs

Adapting AI to LMIC Contexts

  1. Investments in tailoring: Gather high-quality local datasets (language, weather, disease data) to train effective models
  2. Benchmarking as models mature: LMIC implementers need clear ways to judge model accuracy in different contexts
  3. Prerequisites for scale: Consider whether investments can unlock barriers (hardware, internet access) through mechanisms like advanced market commitments

Anticipating Risks & Challenges

  1. Responsible AI considerations: Crucial at intervention level (red teaming, guardrails, human oversight) and systems level (data privacy, algorithmic bias, job loss)
  2. Declining foreign aid environment: Need to drive efficiencies within systems as traditional funding decreases
